THAI × CDP — A Tale of Two Travelers

How a unified customer profile turns an anonymous booking into a personalized, rewarding journey — and the exact steps to demo it.

▶ Open the live demo

The Story

Two people open the same Thai Airways website to book the same Bangkok–London trip. One is a guest — THAI knows nothing about them. The other is Khun Somchai, a Royal Orchid Plus member whose profile is synced from the CDP: his miles, seat preference, favourite meals, and reward eligibility. Same website — completely different journey. That difference is the CDP.

Two Journeys, Side by Side

🟤 The Guest

Not signed in · no data · airline is flying blind
  1. Open the link → homepage loads.
  2. Redeem Miles is locked — “Sign in to use miles.”
  3. Click Search Flights → generic results.
  4. Return flight → focus sits on Economy (cheapest guess, no insight).
  5. Passenger Details → all fields blank, typed by hand.
  6. Extra Services → generic “Select Seat / Select Meal,” no suggestions.
  7. Checkout → a transaction. No recognition, no loyalty.

Takeaway: Functional, but anonymous. Every interaction starts from zero.

🟡 Khun Somchai (ROP + CDP)

Signed in · profile synced from CDP · recognized everywhere
  1. Click Sign In → ROP login pre-fills instantly. CDP: identity
  2. Homepage greets “Khun Somchai · 3,000 ROP Miles” + 3 reward cards. CDP: points balance
  3. Member status follows him on every page (top bar).
  4. Return flight → focus shifts to Royal Silk with a reward nudge. CDP: reward eligibility
  5. Passenger Details → pre-filled + “upgrade to Royal Silk” miles prompt. CDP: profile + contact
  6. Extra Services → seat 12A window, extra legroom & meals Yakiniku / Stir-fried Prawns already set. CDP: seat + meal history
  7. Confirmation → recaps both flights, seats, meals, miles earned. He feels known.

Takeaway: Recognized, anticipated, rewarded — a booking becomes a relationship and a smart upsell.

The Reveal — the ROP Toggle

On any inner page, point to the 🔒 ROP button, then click it → it flips to 🔓 Non-ROP.

Instantly: the name vanishes, fields empty, Royal Silk focus drops back to Economy, personalization disappears — the guest experience. Click again to bring it all back.

“This switch is the CDP turning on and off. Same site, same flight — the only variable is whether we’ve unified and activated the customer’s data. That’s the value, in one click.”
